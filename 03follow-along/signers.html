Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
The principal author of the Declaration of Independence; later became the 3rd President of the United States.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
A strong advocate for independence and later the 2nd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
Renowned inventor, diplomat, and one of the most influential Founding Fathers.
John Hancock (Massachusetts)
Famous for his large, bold signature; served as President of the Continental Congress.
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
Leader of the American Revolution and cousin to John Adams.
Roger Sherman (Connecticut)
The only person to sign all four key U.S. founding documents: the Continental Association, Declaration of Independence, Articles of Confederation, and the Constitution.

Together, these famous signers of the Declaration of Independence represent the courage, vision, and determination that defined the birth of the United States. Each man brought unique strengths—Jefferson’s eloquence, Franklin’s wisdom, Adams’s leadership, Hancock’s boldness, and Sherman’s steady guidance—all united by a shared belief in liberty and self-government. Their decision to risk their lives and reputations for the cause of independence set the foundation for a nation built on freedom and democracy. Their legacy continues to inspire Americans to uphold the values they so bravely declared in 1776.
